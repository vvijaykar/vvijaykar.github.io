When I've Failed

Alain de Botton's TED Talk A Kinder, Gentler Philosophy of Success', discusses the overarching societal ramification of meritocracy, notably the crushing potency of failure. His words eloquently captured an idea that had been churning in my own mind, in a society where those who merit get to the top are praised is the implication that those at the bottom deserve to be there? Often in life, definitions of success and failure are curated by social pressures and not truly our own. The aim of this section is to reframe my own shortcomings and destigmatize the notion of failure, measures of ourselves that aren't always highlighted in a professional or personal matter.

What I've Created


Evaluating Benetech

As a part of my Dimensions of Culture (DOC) requirement at UCSD, I wrote a paper looking at a local community organization and assessing a novel solution to their impact model in the class DOC100D "The Promises and Contradictions of U.S. Culture." The organization that appealed to me the most was Benetech, a Bay-area based non-profit that pioneered software for social good. My paper focused on a critical issue facing nonprofit organizations across the country (grant allocation), and provided a custom solution that not only aligned with the vision and mission of Benetech but also the technological scope of their work. I conceptualized a solution known as Alokate as a grant-matching tool for nonprofits and grant-makers that would increase the transparency in issue areas and automate the process of grant-searching.

Data Dreams

The first project I was apart of during my Data Science Bootcamp at UCSD, my team created an interactive jupyter notebook for our class to utilize and showcased a demo of the project (found in the slides above). The premise was simple: where are the best places for data scientists to work and live. As aspiring data scientists/analyst in the course, we found that it would be most effective to center our project on one of the biggest challenges for people making this transition. We included several different datasets pulled from APIs such as Census, Walkscore, and World Weather Online to answer the most pressing questions for data science job seekers to provide a custom, interactive solution to solving where they would reside.
